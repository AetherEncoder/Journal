Reflection.

 The event titled Big Data and IoT: Paving the Way to Our Future was a Balik Talent Lecture held on August 20, 2024, at 8:30 AM in the ATA Hall. This lecture was organized for the College of Computer Studies students, with the guest speaker being Mr. Abraham Johannes T. Tourbier, BSIT 2020, now a distinguished Senior Big Data Engineer at SmartPlement Singapore. The topic of the lecture was Big Data and IoT, which stands for the Internet of Things.

 During the lecture, I learned that IoT refers to a network of interconnected devices that collect and share data. This data accumulates to such a vast scale that it is termed Big Data, which requires analysis to unlock its potential. Mr. Tourbier shared how his company leverages IoT and Big Data to implement practical solutions, such as controlling lights in large rooms to conserve energy and measuring plant moisture levels to optimize watering and fertilization. These real-world applications showcased how powerful Big Data and IoT can be in solving everyday challenges, making the session highly insightful.

 This lecture expanded my understanding of how information can be utilized not just as a source of knowledge but as a tool to solve complex problems. It emphasized the importance of developing tools capable of analyzing Big Data, as it exceeds human capacity for manual processing. For the first time, I fully understood why the field is called Information Technology—because information has become the most valuable resource of our era. This realization has reshaped my perspective, highlighting the immense potential hidden in data waiting to be harnessed.

 Because of this activity, I have developed a deeper appreciation for data as an invaluable resource with infinite potential. It has inspired me to explore its possibilities and to value the role of technology in transforming raw information into meaningful solutions.

Reflection.

 CodeQuest took place on August 22, 2024, at 1:00 PM in Room CC05. The event was a programming competition with participants forming teams of 1 to 3 members. I participated as part of a team named "Jay and His Servants." Our task was to solve three programming problems of varying difficulty—easy, medium, and hard—using the C language. The experience was intense and challenging, providing a chance to test our programming skills under time constraints.

 When I first read the "easy" problem, I felt optimistic about solving it. However, I stumbled on string comparison, mistakenly thinking the == operator would work. Normally, this would have been the end of our journey. Fortunately, the organizers noticed that all teams were struggling and allowed us to use the internet for reference (AI assistance was still prohibited). This rule change enabled us to make significant progress on the medium problem, although we skipped the easy and hard ones due to time limitations. When the competition ended, we assumed defeat, but surprisingly, we were declared the winners. Despite the victory, I didn't feel any pride; our performance fell far short of excellence.

 This experience reshaped my understanding of the difference between being the best and being truly good. Winning didn't feel like an achievement but rather an acknowledgment that none of the participants—including my team—were prepared enough. It was a humbling reminder of how vast the field of programming is and how much we still need to learn. This competition wasn't a triumph but an eye-opener, pushing me to reflect on my current skill level and the effort required to improve. I now see this event as a stepping stone toward becoming a programmer I can truly be proud of.

 Because of this activity, I have adopted a new mindset: being the best is the bare minimum, while striving for constant improvement is the ultimate goal. This experience has ignited a fire within me to work harder, grow as a programmer, and return to future competitions with skills and confidence worthy of celebration.

Reflection.

 The Guidance and Counseling Group Dynamics Activity took place on August 21 at 2:30 PM and lasted for about an hour and 30 minutes. Held at the ATA Hall, the event was specifically designed for first-year students from the College of Computer Studies, including myself. The main goal was to help us feel more comfortable with one another, easing the nervousness and awkwardness that often come with starting college.

 The activity included engaging games such as Evolution Rock-Paper-Scissors, where players "evolved" into different creatures after winning rounds, and a team-based snake game where participants worked together to protect their team's "tail" while trying to capture another's. Through these activities, I realized that despite not knowing each other, it was possible to collaborate and build rapport in a short amount of time. What I liked most was how the games fostered connections and camaraderie, even among strangers. However, as someone introverted, I found the experience a bit challenging since the activities seemed designed with extroverts in mind.

 This experience significantly impacted my worldview, making me understand that being extroverted isn't as daunting as I had imagined. The presence of a large group made it easier for me to feel comfortable, as attention wasn't focused on any one person. I realized that much of my hesitation toward socializing is mental, and overcoming that mindset could greatly enhance my social skills. I can apply this insight in my daily life by being friendlier and more proactive in starting conversations. Participating in similar activities in the future could help me further overcome my nerves and gradually build confidence in public settings.

 Because of this activity, I am now someone who values building connections and taking initiative. I aim to start small but consistent steps toward improving my social skills and becoming more comfortable in situations that require me to take the lead. This activity has inspired me to embrace growth and step out of my comfort zone.

Reflection.

 The Keyboard Blitz event took place on August 20, 2024, at 2:00 PM and lasted for about an hour. Held in Room CC10, the activity gathered participants and organizers for a competition centered on typing. The objective was simple: to have fun while testing and comparing typing speed and accuracy among competitors. As one of the participants, my role was that of a lead performer, actively competing in the event.

 Initially, I assumed the event would be easy for me. I had always considered myself a fast typist, as my family often complimented my speed. However, my confidence faltered when the organizers announced rules that shifted the focus from speed to accuracy—a glaring weakness of mine. As the competition began, my performance was underwhelming. I ranked among the lowest, even trailing behind participants I had underestimated during warm-ups. This humbled me and made me realize I wasn't as proficient as I had believed.

 This experience deeply impacted my self-perception. I once thought my typing speed made me exceptional, but I realized my proficiency stemmed more from repeated practice than innate talent. This realization sparked a broader understanding: my current skills result from passion and persistence. If I became proficient at typing through enjoyment and repetition, the same principle could apply to other skills I might pursue. This insight redefined my approach to growth and improvement. To become great at something, I must first learn to appreciate and enjoy it.

 Because of this activity, I am now someone who embraces a growth mindset. I may not be a natural genius, but I recognize the potential to become a prodigy through dedication and passion. I feel empowered by the knowledge that I can excel at anything I choose to invest in, making me more confident and open to exploring new interests.

 The Freshie Orientation took place on September 18, 2024, at 8:30 AM in the Luce Auditorium. The event lasted for approximately four hours and aimed to introduce freshmen students to the essential aspects of university life. Topics included guidance and counseling, university policies, anti-sexual harassment guidelines, safe spaces, library services, medical assistance, and more.

 One key takeaway from the orientation was how much the university prioritizes its students' well-being, especially regarding issues like sexual abuse and assault. A significant portion of the program was devoted to these topics, emphasizing their importance. While some attendees might have found this segment lengthy, I appreciated the university's thoroughness. I also enjoyed the intermission performances, such as the Tula Dula, trumpet playing, and the acapella performance. I enjoyed the program so much that I attended the afternoon session for Batch 2 as well. This activity highlighted my value of balancing impatience with the willingness to endure for long-term benefits.

 This experience profoundly changed my perspective. I am now more aware of the consequences and impacts of violating laws related to sexual harassment and safe spaces. The orientation helped me understand the serious repercussions these actions have on individuals and reinforced how critical it is to address them. I feel grateful for the university's dedication to raising awareness on these matters.

 Because of this activity, I have become more knowledgeable about the effects of sexual harassment and the importance of fostering a culture of respect. I am now committed to upholding these values and contributing to a safer and more inclusive environment.

Reflection.

 The TechChika event was organized by DEVCON Philippines and held virtually on October 12, 2024, via Zoom. The session started at 6:00 PM and lasted approximately two hours. As a participant, my role was to listen to the insightful talks and engage in the open forum. The event covered a wide array of tech-related topics, including project management, artificial intelligence (AI), and strategies for thriving in the tech industry.

 During the session, I gained valuable insights. For project management, I learned the importance of balancing time, stakeholders, and budget while maintaining adaptability due to ever-changing requirements. Clear documentation at the project's onset was emphasized to align stakeholders with goals. In the realm of AI, the speakers explained how it simplifies syntax problems and automates processes, offering immense advantages for businesses. However, the discussion also highlighted the risks of over-reliance on tools like language models. AI should serve as an aid, not a crutch, with healthcare and med-tech fields showing promising applications. The overarching lesson was to leverage technology wisely or risk falling behind.

 This experience significantly altered my perspective. It underscored the importance of adaptability in the rapidly evolving tech landscape. To thrive in the next century, one must let go of outdated practices, embrace change, and continuously rebuild skills. At the same time, maintaining integrity and understanding the tools we use are essential. This event inspired me to evolve as a programmer and student, equipping me with a mindset to face challenges with resilience.

 Because of this activity, I am now someone unafraid of transformation. I am willing to dismantle and rebuild my skills and beliefs to achieve greater heights. Starting from zero to reach 100 is no longer daunting—it is a journey I embrace wholeheartedly.

This experience opened my eyes to the realization that our strength, talents, and abilities are gifts from God. It emphasized the importance of committing all our efforts to Him and giving Him the glory. This activity was undeniably beneficial to my personal development, as it deepened my understanding and appreciation of God's influence in my life and the world around me.

 The Galilean Fellowship, held on November 19, 2024, at 5:30 PM. Due to sudden rain, the event, which lasted for over two hours, was moved to the fourth floor of Asuelo Hall. The participants included my classmates from our CHS class, while the instructors were students from the Divinity School. The theme of the event revolved around facing our fears. My role in the activity was as a participant, actively engaging in the discussions and reflections.

 Before the activity, I expected it to involve simply reading a Bible scripture and discussing its lessons. However, the experience turned out to be much deeper and more personal. At one point, we were asked to speak in front of the class, share our fears, and explain our understanding of faith. What I appreciated about the activity was the sense of reflection and the camaraderie it fostered, as well as the bread that was served at the end. However, I disliked that the food didn't reach me, leaving me without a chance to eat. Another frustration was that the instructors arrived over an hour late, which affected the flow of the event.

 Through this activity, I learned that everyone carries their own fears and interprets faith in unique ways. This realization deepened my understanding of how diverse human experiences are. It taught me to view others as individuals with their own stories and struggles, rather than just as people who happen to be around me. I can apply this learning in my interactions by being more mindful and compassionate, recognizing that others have fears and perspectives different from my own. This activity was extremely beneficial in shaping my understanding of human behavior and empathy.

 Because of this activity, I am now more aware that everyone has their own fears and life experiences. I am more compassionate and intentional in being mindful of the people around me.

 This activity was the Ten Toes Down Bible Study, which took place on November 13, 2024, at 6:00 PM. It lasted for about two hours and was held at Freedom Park in Dumaguete. The event was open to everyone—students, passersby, or anyone interested could join. The primary objective of the session was to discuss the teachings of the Bible and spread its message. My role in the activity was that of a participant, listening and reflecting on the discussions shared.

 During the Bible study, we discussed a chapter in Psalms that focused on the “Armor of God.” A key part of the discussion was the concept of idleness—how there are moments in our lives when we act without being fully aware, such as mindlessly scrolling through the internet or inattentively going about daily tasks. The speakers emphasized the importance of always being vigilant and mindful of our actions. What I liked most about the activity was the venue. Being outdoors at Freedom Park, surrounded by nature, created a relaxing and open atmosphere that felt inviting and free.

 This activity profoundly impacted me by making me more self-aware. It highlighted how often I have been idle, failing to fully engage in my actions or surroundings. This realization has motivated me to become more mindful of my decisions and to work on correcting my flaws. I plan to apply this knowledge by being more intentional in my daily actions and by fostering habits that make me more productive and thoughtful. This Bible study was a genuinely enjoyable experience, and I am already planning to attend more sessions like it in the future to further my spiritual and personal growth.

 Because of this activity, I have become more aware of myself and my actions. I now take time to consider whether what I am doing is truly beneficial to me and how I can replace negative habits with more productive and meaningful ones.

It emphasized the importance of not only improving oneself for God but also for those we encounter in life.

 Because of this activity, I am now better equipped to accept life’s challenges and focus on the positives rather than dwelling on the negatives. This newfound outlook has helped me develop a more optimistic and proactive approach to life.
